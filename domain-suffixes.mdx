DNSHE provides four free public domain suffixes, each curated for a different class of project. Whether you are deploying a CI pipeline, a portfolio, a technical blog, or a personal landing page, there is a suffix that fits. All four are available immediately after registration, with no additional verification required.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="*.us.ci" icon="circle-nodes">
    **CI/CD & Automation**

    Designed for infrastructure-focused use cases. Ideal for SaaS platforms, API endpoints, automated testing nodes, and deployment pipelines.

    **Example:** `api.us.ci`, `staging.us.ci`, `webhook.us.ci`
  </Card>
  <Card title="*.cc.cd" icon="paintbrush">
    **Creative & Code**

    Short and memorable. A natural fit for portfolios, creative projects, and open-source repositories you want to share.

    **Example:** `portfolio.cc.cd`, `demo.cc.cd`, `showcase.cc.cd`
  </Card>
  <Card title="*.de5.net" icon="laptop-code">
    **Tech & Engineering**

    A professional feel for technical content. Well-suited to developer blogs, documentation sites, and local dev environments.

    **Example:** `docs.de5.net`, `blog.de5.net`, `dev.de5.net`
  </Card>
  <Card title="*.ccwu.cc" icon="users">
    **Personal & Community**

    Great for personal landing pages, community hubs, and creative showcases that need a memorable, approachable address.

    **Example:** `hello.ccwu.cc`, `community.ccwu.cc`, `about.ccwu.cc`
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  More domain suffixes are available after logging in to the [DNSHE console](https://www.dnshe.com). The four suffixes listed above are the publicly documented free options.
</Note>

## Choosing the right suffix

All four suffixes are backed by the same Anycast DNS infrastructure and support the same full set of record types (A, AAAA, CNAME, MX, TXT, NS, SRV, CAA). The difference is purely semantic — choose based on how you want your subdomain to read and what audience you are addressing.

<Tip>
  Pick a suffix that reflects the purpose of your project. A suffix like `us.ci` signals infrastructure to technical readers, while `ccwu.cc` reads as personal and approachable. Choosing the right suffix makes your subdomain easier to remember and communicate.
</Tip>
